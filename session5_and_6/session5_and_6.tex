\subsection{Pre-class Activities}

The pre-class activity requires you to engage in preliminary analysis and information gathering to prepare for product planning discussions.

\subsubsection{Activity 1: Market Analysis and Planning Elements}
\textbf{Objective:} \
The objective of this activity is to analyze key elements for product planning, including market segments, technology integration, manufacturing and service objectives, target market $\&$ price, and assumptions $\&$ constraints. This analysis will help in identifying promising opportunities and effectively managing your project portfolio.
\vspace{10pt}

\noindent
To prepare for product planning, address the following questions:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Market Segments:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Identify and list potential market segments that should be considered when designing the product and its features.
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Technology Integration:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Explore and identify new technologies, if any, that should be integrated into the new product.
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Manufacturing and Service:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Define the manufacturing and service objectives and constraints that will impact product development.
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Target Market and Price:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Clearly define the primary and secondary target market(s) for the product.
\item Establish a realistic target price range for the product in the defined market(s).
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Assumptions, Constraints, and Stakeholders:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Outline key assumptions underlying your product plan.
\item Identify any constraints that may limit product development or market entry.
\item List the key stakeholders involved in the product planning process.
\end{enumerate}
\vspace{10pt}
\textbf{Instructions:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Conduct independent research and brainstorming to address each of the points above for your chosen product or product idea.
\item Document your initial thoughts, findings, and preliminary answers for each question.
\item Be prepared to share and discuss your analysis with your team members during the in-class activity.
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Activity 2: Mission Statement Draft}
\textbf{Objective:} \
The objective of this activity is to draft a preliminary mission statement for your product, incorporating key details derived from your market analysis and planning considerations.
\vspace{10pt}

\noindent
To draft a mission statement, include the following details:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Product Description:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Provide a concise description of the product itself.
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Benefit Proposition:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Clearly articulate the key benefit(s) the product offers to its target market(s).
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Key Business Goals:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item State the primary business goals the product is intended to achieve (e.g., market share, revenue, profitability).
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Primary Market:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Identify the primary target market segment for the product.
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Secondary Markets:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Identify any secondary market segments that the product may also address.
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Assumptions and Constraints:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Briefly list the key assumptions and constraints identified in Activity 1 that are relevant to the mission statement.
\end{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Stakeholders:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item List the primary stakeholders who are relevant to the product's success.
\end{enumerate}
\vspace{10pt}
\textbf{Instructions:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Based on your analysis from Activity 1, draft a preliminary mission statement that incorporates all the details listed above.
\item Prepare to share and refine your mission statement draft with your team members during the in-class activity.
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{In-class Activities}
\subsubsection{Activity 1: Collaborative Refinement of Market Analysis and Planning Elements}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Market Analysis and Planning Discussion:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Share and discuss individual findings from Pre-class Activity 1 regarding market segments, technology integration, manufacturing and service objectives, target market \& price, and assumptions \& constraints.
\item Engage in a collaborative discussion to refine and consolidate the analysis based on the diverse perspectives and insights of team members.
\item Work towards reaching a consensus on the most relevant market segments, technologies, manufacturing considerations, target market \& price, and key assumptions \& constraints for your product plan.
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Activity 2: Finalizing the Mission Statement through Team Collaboration}
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Mission Statement Development and Finalization:}
\begin{enumerate}
\item Review and discuss the preliminary mission statement drafts prepared in Pre-class Activity 2 by each team member.
\item Through collaborative discussion, combine the best elements from individual drafts and refine the mission statement to create a unified and comprehensive version.
\item Ensure the final mission statement clearly articulates all required details: product description, benefit proposition, key business goals, primary market, secondary markets, assumptions and constraints, and stakeholders.
\item Confirm that the finalized mission statement reflects the collective agreement of all team members and provides a clear direction for further product planning activities.
\end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Remark (Optional - For Extra Consideration or extra mark)}
To demonstrate a deeper understanding and potentially earn extra mark be it for individual or group submission, you can add remarks that provide further justification, context, or insights to your analysis in either the pre-class or in-class activities.

\textbf{Guidelines for Remarks:}

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Detailed Justification:} If you make a specific choice or assumption in your analysis (e.g., selecting a particular target market, choosing a specific technology), provide a detailed justification for this decision. Explain why this is a suitable choice and support your reasoning.
\item \textbf{Reference/Citation:} Back up your justifications or insights with credible sources. This could include:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Internet Sources}: Cite reputable websites, industry reports, market research data, or news articles relevant to your product or market. Ensure the sources are credible (e.g., Statista, Gartner, industry-specific publications, academic institutions).
\item \textbf{Journal Articles/Academic Research:} If applicable, cite relevant academic articles or research papers that support your analysis or provide theoretical frameworks. You can use databases like Google Scholar, JSTOR, or IEEE Xplore to find relevant publications.
\item \textbf{Textbook or Course Materials:} Refer to concepts, frameworks, or examples discussed in your textbook or other course materials to strengthen your arguments.
\end{itemize}
\item \textbf{Beyond the Basics:} Remarks should go beyond simply answering the basic questions. They should demonstrate critical thinking, industry awareness, and a deeper engagement with the product planning process. Examples of valuable remarks could include:
\begin{itemize}
\item Discussing potential risks or challenges associated with your chosen market segment or technology.
\item Analyzing the competitive landscape and how it influences your product planning decisions.
\item Exploring relevant industry trends and how they might impact your product's success.
\item Justifying your chosen target price point based on market research or competitor analysis.
\item Explaining the rationale behind prioritizing certain stakeholders over others.
\end{itemize}
\item \textbf{Proper Citation Format:} Use a consistent citation format (i.e, APA) when referencing external sources. Even informal internet sources should be clearly linked or described.

\end{enumerate}
