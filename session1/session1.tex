\subsection{In-class Activities}
\subsubsection{Activity 1}
The objective of this activity is to walk you through the process of selecting a product for the mock development process as well as determine the company under which the product will be developed. At the end of the activity, your group should establish a clear direction for developing the chosen product within the specified company. Accuracy is not a priority at this stage, so you only need to draft in bullet point format.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item During the discussion, decide on the product that your group intends to develop, consider the market demand, feasibility, and the group's capabilities in developing the product.

    You can select any product you want to design,
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item but it is recommended to select something the team members like and know well and have interest to develop.
        \item The product can be an original  (please, check Google)! OR a derivative of an existing product. But for simplicity, better find something that already available on the market
        \item We can discuss the suitability of the product, but your team is responsible for selecting a product whose design is feasible given the constraints of the course and the requirements for this project.
        \item While products can take various forms, this activity specifically emphasizes engineered, discrete, and physical products.
    \end{enumerate}
    \item Determine the company under which this product will be developed.
    In this project, we assume each group affiliated with a firm and you should envision yourself as part of the chosen company.
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item For example, if your team chose to develop a smartphone (such as the Samsung Galaxy S series), you should imagine yourself as a Samsung employee.
        \item The chosen firm can fall into one of two potential scenarios:
        \begin{itemize}
            \item being a successful, well-established company that has developed the chosen product, or
            \item being a small company aiming to enter the market for that product.
        \end{itemize}
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Activity 2} % if available
For those of you who are willing to paddle the extra mile, there are several sub-activities that can be conducted further. It should be noted that these activities are not required to be completed during class, and some of them may take several days to finish. The activities can help the team in selecting the product to be develop or may even serve as the foundation for your coursework project.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Draft the following which is related to customer needs identification:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Identify the specific needs that the product aims to address.
        \item Identify how the product will provide a solution to the identified customer needs.
    \end{itemize}
    \item Discuss and outline the design of the product:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Specify the design requirements and parameters.
    \end{itemize}
    \item Estimate the number of parts required for the product.
    \begin{itemize}
        \item create a rough sketch of the design, if possible.
    \end{itemize}
    \item Determine the sales price for the product:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Consider the manufacturing cost, desired profit margin, and market competition.
    \end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}
