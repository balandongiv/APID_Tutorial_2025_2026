\subsection{Compulsory Elements}
Your report must include the following key elements, as these will be considered in grading:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Report Title}
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Provide a clear, concise title accurately reflecting the content of the report.
    \end{enumerate}

    \item \textbf{Introduction}
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item The introduction should concisely and comprehensively outline the issue your product aims to address and explain why the product is necessary.
        \item Provide an overview of the main sections of the report to clearly indicate the content that follows.
    \end{enumerate}

    \item \textbf{Opportunity Identification}
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item This section is pivotal in your product development process and should include your proposed innovation charter.
        \item List all opportunities identified based on your group's innovation charter.
    \end{enumerate}

    \item \textbf{Product Planning}
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Since the opportunity funnel may initially capture numerous ideas, many may not align well with your firm's goals or capabilities. It is therefore crucial to identify and select the most promising opportunities. To manage your project portfolio effectively, evaluate competitive strategies, market segmentation, and technological trajectories. Specifically, address the following:
        \begin{enumerate}
            \item Which market segments should be considered when designing the product and its features?
            \item What new technologies, if any, should be integrated into the new product?
            \item What are the manufacturing and service objectives and constraints?
            \item Clearly define the target market(s) and establish a target price.
            \item Outline assumptions, constraints, and stakeholders involved in the planning process.
        \end{enumerate}
        \item Based on these analyses, create a mission statement that includes the following details:
        \begin{enumerate}
            \item Product description
            \item Benefit proposition
            \item Key business goals
            \item Primary market
            \item Secondary markets
            \item Assumptions and constraints
            \item Stakeholders
        \end{enumerate}
    \end{enumerate}

    \item \textbf{Development Processes and Organization}
    \begin{enumerate}
        \item Propose an appropriate product development process, define an optimal process flow, and suggest a suitable organizational structure. Briefly justify your selections.
    \end{enumerate}
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Concept Generation}

Document your selected method for decomposing the problem into key components or subsystems. Choose one of the following methods:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Functional Decomposition
    \item Decomposition by Sequence of User Actions
    \item Decomposition by Key Customer Needs
\end{enumerate}

Then:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item Compile at least five potential solutions for each critical sub-problem, drawing from internal and external sources, reflecting the team's consensus.
    \item Construct a concept classification tree and concept combination table.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Concept Testing}
Hypothetically, if the course were extended, briefly explain your approach and methods for concept testing. Describe how you would validate your concepts through surveys or interviews and select the most promising one based on collected data.

\subsubsection{Prototyping Plan}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Describe the importance of prototyping and outline your prototyping plan.
    \item Define the objectives and steps for creating your prototype(s).
\end{enumerate}
